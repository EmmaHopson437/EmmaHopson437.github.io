Astronauts

I need a space

NASA currently has 44 astronauts. Of those 44, 14 of them are women. Astronauts have to go through a rigorous process to be picked. They have to first be admitted into the astronaut candidate trianing program and then they are selected from there. Even if you don't get picked to be an astronaut you are still almost guaranteed a position working for NASA. Here is a list of some of the active and former astronauts.

Active Astronauts

Christopher J Cassidy

Cassidy was formly a U.S. Navy SEAL. He was picked by NASA in 2004 and has been on two space fligts. Cassidy was a part of STS-127 and Expedition 35. For mission STS-127 he served as mission specialist.
Christopher went through the U.S. Naval Academy and MIT. He has a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics and a Master of Science in Ocean Engineering.

Victor Glover
Victor was picked to be an astronaut in 2013 after being a Naval Aviator. He was a test pilot for the F/A‐18 Hornet, Super Hornet and EA‐18G Growler.
Glover went to college at California Polytechnic State University, Air University and the Naval Postgraduate School. He has a Bachelor of Science in General Engineering, Master of Science in Flight Test Engineering, Master of Science in Systems Engineering, and a Master of Military Operational Art and Science.

Christina Koch
Christina was picked by NASA to be an astronaut in 2013. She finished the astronaut candidate training program in 2015 and was assigned to the International Space Station Crew Operations branch.
Koch graduated from North Carolina State University with a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering and a Bachelor of Science in Physics and a Master of Science in Electrical Engineering.

Scott Tingle
Scott Tingle was selected in 2009 to be an astronaut. He spent three years with the Aerospace Corporation and then was commissioned a naval officer in 1991. He was launched to the International Space Station in December of 2017.
Tingle attended Southeastern Massachusetts University, and Purdue University. He earned his Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering with a specialty in fluid mechanics and propulsion and Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering.

Sunita Williams
Sunita Williams was picked in 1998 to be an astronaut for NASA and is a veteran of two space missions Expeditions. She was on 14/15 and 32/33. She is currently working with commercial providers to make new spacecraft systems.
Williams attended the U.S. Naval Academy and Florida Institute of Technology. She graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Physical Science and Master of Science in Engineering Management.

Former Astronauts

Buzz Aldrin
Buzz Aldrin was a part of the third group of astronauts to be selected by NASA. We was the lunar module pilot for the Apollo 11 mission. He was also launched in the Gemini 12 spacecraft.
Aldrin graduated from the U.S. Military Academy at West Point in 1951 with a bachelor of science. He also attended MIT and recieved his doctorate of science in Astronautics

Neil Armstrong
Armstrong was a research pilot for NASA and flew over 200 different aircraft. Armstrong could fly jets, helicopters, gliders and rockets. He was command pilot for the Gemini 8 project and commander for Apollo 11.
Neil Armstrong attended Purdue University where he got his Bachelor of Science in Aeronautical Engineering. He also recieved his Master of Science in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Southern California.

John H Casper
Casper has over 825 logged hours in space and is a veteran of four space missions. He was selected by NASA in 1984 after he was fighter pilot who flew 229 combat missions in the 35th Tactical Fighter Wing.
John Casper attended the U.S. Air Force Academy where he got his Bachelor of Science degree in Engineering Science.He also recieved a Master of Science degree in Astronautics from Purdue University.

Linda M Godwin
Godwin first joined NASA in 1980 in the Payload Operations Division where she worked on payload integration. She was selected to be an astronaut candidate in 1985 and became an astronaut in 1986. Godwin has over 38 days logged in Space and over 10 hours of EVA logged. She is a veteran of four space missions.
Linda Godwin graduated from Southeast Missouri State and got her bachelor of science degree in mathematics and physics from She also went to the University of Missouri and got a master of science degree and a doctorate in physics.

Scott J Kelly
Scott Kelly was selected by NASA in 1996 and was assigned technical duties in the Astronaut Office Spacecraft Systems/Operations branch. Scott Kelly holds the record for time in orbit by a U.S. astronaut.
Kelly has a Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering from the State University of New York Maritime College. He also recieved his Master of Science degree in Aviation Systems from the University of Tennessee.
