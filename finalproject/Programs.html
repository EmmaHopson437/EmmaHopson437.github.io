Aeronautics Academy

This program is offered to students in higher education and is available nationally.

This is a team based program that allows college students to research and be exposed to what NASA does. It also works on developing leadership skills.

To qualify for the Aeronautics Academy you must be between your third year in college and second year in graduate school with a 3.2 GPA or better. People must also be US citizens.

Dropping in a Microgravity Environment

Offered grades 5-12 and is available nationally.

This is a competition that allows students to work on a team and create a science experiment that will be tested at a NASA microgravity drop tower facility.

In order to become a team students must think of a scientific concept and write a proposal for an experiment and send it to NASA. All work should be done by the students, not the advisors.

FIRST Robotics

This program is offered grades 9-12 and is available nationally.

FIRST stands for, For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology. It is a competition that allows students to work with major business and industry as they create a robot for a two minute competition.

For more information visit this website: FIRST Robotics

NASA Aeronautics Scholarship Program

This is offered to Higher Education students Nationally.

The NASA Office of Education offers scholarships to undergraduate students who are persuing degrees in NASA related STEM. NASA also has Graduates degree that focuses in giving students interested in aeronautical research scholarships. Their hope is to reach out to college students who are highly motivated and interested in NASA missions and research.

To apply for these students should visit the NASA website. NASA may not do this every year so they have to see if this is a continuing thing.

NASA Human Exploration Rover Challenge

This program is both national and international for grades 9 through higher education.

The NASA Human Exploration Rover Challenge is meant for students to design and build mobility devices that can opporate on surfaces in space. Such as the moon, comets, and other planets. It is created for students who could potentially be part of these missions in the future.

For more information visit the website, Human Exploration Rover Challenge
