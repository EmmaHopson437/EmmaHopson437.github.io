Here is a brief history of things that NASA has done and how it was created. This goes from the 1950s through the 1990s.

I need a space

1950s

  • April 2, 1958 President Dwight D. Eisenhower proposed NASA to a joint Congress
  • On June 2, 1958 the House passed a NASA bill
  • June 16 Senate passed their bill. The bills were passed mostly because the Soviet Union had another successful launch of Sputnik 3
  • NASA opened on October 1, 1958
  • October 11, 1958 is NASA's first launch of Pioneer 1
  • December 18, 1958 President Eisenhower send the first voice from space when he sent out a Christmas message from PROJECT SCORE, a communication satellite.
  • March 3, 1959 NASA sent Pioneer 4 to the moon and it was the first successful lunar flyby

1960s

  • April 1, 1960 NASA launched the first meteorological satellite which could monitor earth's weather
  • December 19, 1960 Mercury 1 launched which was the first in a series, Mercury was unmanned
  • May 6, 1961 Freedom 7 was launched which was the first American launch with a human being. Alan Shepard was in suborbital orbit for 15 minutes
  • May 25, 1961 President John F Kennedy said that he thinks that America should send a man to the moon before the decade is over
  • February 12, 1962 Glen orbited the earth three times in the Friendship 7 a mercury rocket. Even with technological difficulties he was able to land safely and make the last two orbits manually
  • July 10, 1962 Telstar I was launched which was the first privately made satellite and it was the first TV and telephone signals made by satellite.
  • December 14, 1962 The first planetary flyby was made past Venus
  • July 14, 1965 First close up pictures of Mars by Mariner 4
  • March 16,1966 Gemini VIII docked with another space craft which was a key concept in the upcoming lunar project
  • June 2, 1966 Surveyor I had landed on the lunar surface a provided lunar data for weeks
  • January 27, 1967 the Apollo Saturn went up in flames killing three astronauts. These were the first deaths linked directly to space flight
  • July 16-24, 1968 Apollo 11 touched down on the lunar surface and Neil Armstrong set foot on the moon

1970s

  • April 11-17, 1970 Apollo 13 had an oxygen tank rupture that caused several systems aboard to stop working. The astronauts on board had to return to earth in the landing module because the original craft was insufficient. This was a close call for NASA.
  • July 26- August 7, 1971 NASA sent Apollo 15 which had the first lunar rover and was able to extend their search. They broght back a piece from the ancient crust and called it the "Genesis Rock"
  • November 13, 1971 Mariner 9 orbited Mars. This mission was the first to orbit another planet
  • March 3, 1972 Pioneer 10 was launched into the solar system to get a better look at the gas giants
  • July 23, 1972 Landsat I was launched which changed the way that people saw the earth. Landsat I gave information about vegetation and other land data
  • July 15-24, 1975 Apollo-Soyuz Test Project was launched. Apollo-Soyuz was a joint project between the US and the Soviet Union. This opened up the idea of joint missions with the Soviet Union.
  • August 20, 1975 Viking I was launed and sent to Mars. It landed on July 20, 1976.
  • August 12, 1977 the first Space Shuttle had its first free test flight. This ship is called Enterprise

1980s

  • February 14, 1980 Solar Maximum was launched to gather in depth information about the sun.
  • June 18, 1983 Sally K. Ride became the first women astronaut. She was one of the mission specialists aboard the Challenger
  • January 25, 1984 President Reagan made the announcement that he wanted to create a space station by the end of the decade. He proposed his idea infront of Congress as a part of the State of the Union Address.
  • January 28, 1986 the Space Shuttle Challenger blew up killing all seven members aboard. This was very significant because the members abaord represented all different type of people. This was a big hit on NASA and they had a hard time trying to recover from this horrible accident
  • August 15, 1986 President Ronald Reagan announced that no more commercial sattelites would be launched unless they had national security in mind
  • October 18, 1989 Galileo launched to go to Jupiter to get more in depth information of the atmosphere

1990s

  • April 24, 1990 Hubble Space Telescope was launched and has been taking pictures up to present day
  • August 13, 1996 Galileo found warm ice or even liquid water on Europa, one of Jupiter's moons. This is significant because water is the key element of life
  • January 13, 1997 NASA scientists discovered three black holes in galaxies. This suggested that many common galaxies have black holes
  • July 4, 1997 the Mars Pathfinder landed on Mars and collected 1.2 gigabits of information and sent over 10,000 photos of the Martian serface
  • May 28, 1998 gave Earth the first image of a plant outside of the solar system. This planet was thought to have been flung outside of orbit from it's star.
